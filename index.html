About

My name is Si-Yue Yu (余 思悦), born in Haifeng County, Shanwei City, Guangdong Province, China. I got my B.S. in physics and a minor in astronomy from Xiamen University in 2014. I am currently a Ph.D. candidate in astrophysics working with Professor Luis C. Ho at Kavli Institute for Astronomy and Astrophysics (KIAA), Peking University.

Research and Interests

I mainly work on galaxy structures, such as spirals, bar, and lopsidedness, and their connections with global galaxy properties. I am particularly intertesed in the effects of spiral arms on the star formation on both galactic and sub-galactic scale.
I also work on spirals in protoplanetary disk. I study the origin of these spiral arms by examing the relationship between arm properties and disk properties. I am particularly interested in using spiral arms as a tool to probe the disk evolution.
